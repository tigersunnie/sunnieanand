A Note from the City of Angels

Sunnie · Los Angeles

Greetings friends and colleagues,

I've been heads-down for a few months — deep in the studio, deep in a new city, deep in a creative chapter I've been building toward for a long time. This felt like the right moment to surface, say hello, and catch you up.

The short version: I'm now based in sunny LA — and yes, the name fits.

A quick note on the name: many of you know me as Shelley. Sunnie is a nickname that goes back to preschool — a teacher called me Sunshine, it stuck, and over time it simply felt more like me. As I stepped into this creative chapter, I chose to use Sunnie Anand for my artwork, creative projects, and professional work. Nothing dramatic — I still happily answer to Shelley or Sunnie. Just wanted to connect the dots in case you landed here and wondered.

The longer version is below, and I promise it's worth the scroll.

What I've been building

After many rewarding years in corporate sustainability, I'm dedicating this chapter to the work that has always run alongside everything else — art, curation, slow fashion, writing, and the kind of community-building that happens in living rooms and galleries rather than conference rooms.

This season has given me the opportunity to go deep on my creative practice, pursue long-held artistic goals, and explore new ways of bringing together the worlds I've always loved: creativity, sustainability, culture, and social impact.

Today, my work includes:

Fine art and creative practice — twenty years of studio work across painting, ink, watercolor, collage, and large-scale installation. I show up every morning and make something. It adds up. See the full body of work →

Art curation and cultural projects — bespoke gallery talks, museum tours, art walks, private art advisory, and exhibition development. I've logged 80+ world-class museums across four continents and have very strong opinions about all of them. Learn more →

Sustainable fashion and circular design — garment construction, material sourcing, costume design, and personal styling rooted in slow fashion values. The fashion fairies are real and they lead me straight to the good stuff. Explore →

Consulting and advisory — available for select engagements in sustainability, systems change, circular economy, and mission-driven organizations. The corporate operator brain didn't go anywhere — it just comes with a fuller perspective now.

Speaking, writing, and community — including essays on art, sustainability, and culture and intimate Sunnie Salons in Los Angeles — invitation-only evenings around art, ideas, and good conversation.
